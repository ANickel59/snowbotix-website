Our Story

RoboWorkX began with a practical problem.

Outdoor maintenance still depends on people operating equipment in conditions where the work is repetitive, difficult to staff, and sometimes dangerous. Snow-covered sidewalks. Icy entrances. Steep mowing areas. Debris after storms. Long routes that need to be cleared, checked, and documented.

These are ordinary problems until they fail. Then they become safety issues, access issues, service issues, and liability issues.

Snowbotix was built to address that kind of work. The first version focused on snow removal for non-road environments such as sidewalks, plazas, entryways, campuses, and commercial routes. As the company tested the system, the need widened. The same customers also needed help with sweeping, mowing, brush, deicing, and recurring outdoor maintenance.

That changed the product. Snowbotix moved from a single-season robot to a year-round autonomous maintenance platform. It also clarified the company's long-term direction. The most important part of the system is the autonomy layer: sensing, compute, navigation, obstacle detection, route execution, supervision, and reporting. RoboWorkX calls that layer AIB, Autonomy-in-a-Box.

Snowbotix is the first commercial application. AIB is the technology foundation behind it.

Our Philosophy

Supervised Autonomy

Autonomy should support the people responsible for the work. The robot performs defined tasks, while people remain involved in planning, deployment, monitoring, service, and judgment.

That human layer matters. Outdoor work is variable. Sites are different. Weather changes. Customers need confidence that the system is being watched, supported, and improved. RoboWorkX is building for that reality.
