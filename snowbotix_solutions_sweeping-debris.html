01 — The Operational Problem

Sweeping looks simple until it becomes constant.

Sand, salt, leaves, litter, dirt, and surface buildup return across the same walkways, curb lines, parking edges, campus paths, and public-facing entrances. When teams are short-staffed, these routes get pushed behind higher-priority work. The result is visible deterioration: messy entrances, uneven campus appearance, harder-to-maintain pedestrian routes, and more manual cleanup hours.

For large sites, the issue is not whether someone can sweep. The issue is whether the work can be repeated, scheduled, verified, and kept off the critical path for already-stretched facilities teams.

  • Recurring routes compete with urgent work orders
  • Post-winter sand and salt cleanup stretches across large surface areas
  • Leaves, litter, and debris return faster than teams can clear them
  • Public-facing entrances need cleaner surfaces before peak arrival windows
  • Facilities leaders have limited visibility into what was actually completed
  • Contracted work can be difficult to verify without route-level documentation

04 — Operating Model

Configured for the route. Supervised in operation.

Snowbotix starts with site assessment and route mapping. Approved paths are geofenced and programmed into the operating system. The robot then runs scheduled or on-demand sweeping missions under remote human supervision.

Attachments

Power broom and debris configurations

Snowbotix can be configured with a power broom (50–60 in.) for sweeping and surface cleanup. Vacuum-oriented configurations are available where appropriate. Final attachment selection depends on surface type, debris profile, route width, access constraints, and collection requirements.

Sensing

360-degree obstacle detection throughout

The robot uses full perimeter sensing to detect people, vehicles, obstacles, and route obstructions. When an obstruction prevents completion, the system stops, alerts a human supervisor, and creates a record for follow-up.

Autonomous mode

Mapped recurring routes

Robot follows pre-mapped routes within defined geofenced zones at 2 mph. Human supervisor monitors remotely via fleet portal with full override capability.

Line-of-sight mode

Repositioning and complex maneuvers

Used for crew-assisted loading, repositioning between routes, or navigating tight approach paths. Requires trained operator in direct line of sight. Up to 5–6 mph.

06 — Site Fit

See whether Snowbotix fits your sweeping routes.

Strong fit

  • Commercial, institutional, municipal, industrial, energy, aviation, or government sites
  • Recurring sweeping or debris routes
  • Defined sidewalks, paths, plazas, edges, lots, yards, or controlled outdoor zones
  • Need for scheduled, repeatable, documented maintenance
  • Labor shortages or high-value crews pulled into low-complexity cleanup
  • Ability to support site mapping, staging, charging, and operations review
  • Surface conditions suitable for a tracked commercial robot

Likely not a fit

  • Residential properties
  • One-time cleanup projects
  • Loose, undefined, or constantly changing work areas
  • Hazardous material cleanup
  • Heavy waste removal or bulk dumping
  • High-speed public road sweeping
  • Sites without a workable staging, charging, or supervision model
  • Routes with crowd conditions that prevent predictable autonomous operation
