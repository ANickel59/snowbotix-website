01 — The Operational Problem

Outdoor maintenance does not happen in one season.

Large sites need recurring outdoor work across every season: sidewalks cleared, paths swept, debris removed, slopes maintained, vegetation controlled, assets inspected, and work documented. The problem is that most maintenance models are fragmented. One crew handles snow. Another handles mowing. A contractor handles sweeping. A separate process handles inspection. Reporting sits in texts, photos, spreadsheets, work orders, or vendor logs.

That creates a year-round operating problem.

  • Crews are stretched across recurring low-complexity routes
  • Seasonal equipment sits idle between use cases
  • Vendors create inconsistent visibility across sites and seasons
  • Hard-to-staff routes are delayed, skipped, or handled reactively
  • Inspection and maintenance records are separated from the work itself
  • Facilities leaders lack one operating view across outdoor conditions, route completion, and follow-up needs

Snowbotix consolidates recurring outdoor work into one autonomous maintenance platform.

06 — Site Fit

Best for sites with recurring outdoor work across multiple seasons.

Strong fit

  • Commercial, institutional, municipal, industrial, energy, aviation, or government sites
  • Recurring outdoor maintenance routes
  • Multiple seasonal needs across snow, sweeping, mowing, vegetation, inspection, or reporting
  • Defined sidewalks, paths, plazas, slopes, yards, lots, corridors, or controlled zones
  • Labor shortages or hard-to-staff routes
  • Need for route-level service records
  • Ability to support mapping, staging, charging, and operations review
  • Internal owner to coordinate facilities, operations, or public works stakeholders

Likely not a fit

  • Residential properties
  • One-time cleanup jobs
  • Sites with no recurring outdoor maintenance route
  • Indoor-only maintenance needs
  • High-speed public road sweeping or roadway-only work
  • Bulk waste removal or hazardous material cleanup
  • Sites without a practical staging, charging, or supervision model
  • Routes that change too frequently to support repeatable autonomous operation

Not sure whether your site qualifies? Share a route map, aerial image, service zone, or current maintenance workflow. Snowbotix will review the fit.
