Technology Specifications / Fleet Management Portal

A single command view for autonomous outdoor maintenance.

The Snowbotix Fleet Management Portal gives your team three working views: schedule deployments in advance, monitor live missions with GPS, video, and battery state of charge, and pull proof-of-service reports for every completed route. Responsive across desktop, tablet, and mobile.

Scheduler / Monitor / Report Live GPS position tracking Low-latency live video Battery state of charge (SoC) Hazard alert and review workflow Proof-of-service reporting GIS export and API access Desktop, tablet & mobile

01 — Dashboard Features

Everything a supervisor needs to oversee an active outdoor maintenance mission.

The portal is organized around three working views. Scheduler plans future deployments, assigns robots to operational zones, and manages seasonal transitions. Monitor tracks active missions — live GPS location, low-latency video streams, mechanical status, and battery state of charge. Report generates geo-tagged, timestamped proof-of-service records for every completed route.

Live Operations

Robot position and route visualization

Live map view showing current robot position, active route geometry, exclusion zones, and mission progress in real time.

Status Monitoring

Battery, speed, and attachment state

Live battery state of charge (SoC), estimated remaining runtime, current operating speed, attachment status, and mission elapsed time.

Live Video

360° camera view

Live access to onboard camera feeds for real-time visual supervision without requiring on-site presence during autonomous operation.

Dispatch

Mission dispatch and scheduling

Manual and scheduled dispatch from the portal or Fleet App. Mission queue management across multiple robots and routes.

Weather

Hyper-local weather scheduling

Weather data integrated into dispatch scheduling for storm-triggered automatic missions, deicing threshold activation, and hold/resume decisions.

Maintenance

Predictive maintenance analytics

Usage tracking, component condition indicators, and scheduled maintenance alerts based on runtime hours, cycle counts, and operating conditions.

03 — Hazard Alert Workflow

Six steps from detection to record.

01

Detect

360° sensing identifies object intrusion within the 6-foot safety buffer during autonomous operation.

02

Capture

Robot stops immediately. Sensor snapshot taken: GPS position, timestamp, camera captures, and sensor readings locked to event record.

03

Alert

Push notification dispatched to supervisor via fleet portal and Fleet App. On-robot visual and audio alerts active at site.

04

Review

Supervisor reviews event: camera capture, GPS location, sensor data. Determines whether to resume, redirect, or hold mission.

05

Notify

Supervisor updates site team as needed. On-site crew can be dispatched to clear obstacle or assist with complex maneuver if required.

06

Record

Event logged permanently to mission record: time, location, sensor state, camera captures, supervisor response, and outcome. Available in proof-of-service report export.

04 — Role-Based Access

A strict role hierarchy: Organization Owner, Admin, Crew.

Not every user needs full fleet control. The portal enforces a strict role hierarchy so each user only has access to the controls and data relevant to their clearance level — preventing unauthorized route changes and keeping accountability clear across your team.

RoleAccess scope
Organization OwnerFull account control: user management, route management, system configuration, export controls, API credentials
AdminDay-to-day fleet operation: scheduling, live monitoring, dispatch, hazard review, mission override, proof-of-service reports, GIS exports
CrewField access: live status view, on-site alert receipt, physical E-stop operation — no administrative controls

Read-only visibility for stakeholders such as facility managers, compliance officers, or GIS analysts is configured within these roles during deployment setup.

05 — Proof-of-Service Reporting

Every completed mission produces a documented record.

Geo-tagged, timestamped proof-of-service reports document exactly what was serviced, where, and when — the record your team needs for SLA compliance, billing verification, and liability documentation in a managed service model.

Record elementWhat it contains
Mission GPS traceFull route geometry from mission start to completion, timestamped per position point
Geotagged mediaCamera captures at key route points, obstacle events, surface conditions, and periodic interval snapshots
Event logAll obstacle detections, stops, pauses, overrides, battery events, and mission interruptions with timestamps and GPS coordinates
Material application recordWhere and when deicing or other materials were applied during the mission
Surface condition logSensor-detected surface observations recorded along the route
Mission summaryTotal time, distance, area covered, attachments used, and completion status

07 — Accessibility & Conformance

Section 508 compliant hardware. A published roadmap for portal accessibility.

The SMUR hardware platform is fully compliant with US Section 508 requirements. The fleet management portal already supports baseline visual contrast, text scaling, and keyboard navigation, and Snowbotix is executing a dedicated remediation roadmap to bring it into full WCAG 2.1 A and AA conformance — with dates, not vague intentions.

PhaseScopeStatus
Accessibility audit & gap analysisInternal review of contrast, text scaling, keyboard navigation, semantic structure, and ARIA labelingCompleted
Core dashboard remediationAlt-text for dynamic elements, accessible text formats for map and telemetry layers, structured data tables for battery, payload, and mission statusIn progress — Q3 2026
Screen reader & assistive technology supportNVDA and JAWS on desktop; VoiceOver and TalkBack on tablet and mobile. Operational alerts, safety notifications, and telemetry read aloud in real timeScheduled — Q4 2026
Validation & conformance reportUsability testing with accessibility professionals and native assistive-technology users, followed by a formal Accessibility Conformance Report (VPAT 2.5 Rev 508)Scheduled — Q1 2027
