Asset Inspection & Site Intelligence

Every maintenance route is also a sensing pass.

Snowbotix is equipped with a fused sensing stack on every mission — RTK-GNSS positioning, HD cameras, thermal imaging, LiDAR, and radar. As the robot maintains a route, it captures site conditions, detects obstructions, logs hazards, and surfaces exceptions for human review. Every deployment creates a geocoded operating record that shows what the robot saw and what needs follow-up.

01 — The Problem

Outdoor sites change faster than teams can inspect them.

Pavement conditions, surface damage, drainage issues, fallen materials, access obstructions, vegetation problems, and signage failures accumulate between manual walkthroughs. On large or distributed sites with recurring outdoor maintenance, the gap between condition changes and human awareness can be days or weeks. By the time an issue is noticed and escalated, it has often grown into a more expensive repair or created a disruption.

Snowbotix does not replace a dedicated inspection program. What it does is use every maintenance route as an additional pass — capturing conditions, logging events, and creating a geocoded record of what was observed along the route without adding a separate crew or separate schedule.

02 — The Sensing Stack

A fused sensing stack active during every mission.

Snowbotix operates with a layered sensing stack designed for navigation, obstacle detection, and environmental awareness. All sensors are active and logging during every autonomous mission. Inspection capability is not an add-on — it is a byproduct of the same sensing system used for navigation and safety.

Positioning

RTK-GNSS

High-precision positioning for location-tagged records at every point along the route

Tactical IMU

Inertial measurement

Orientation and movement tracking that complements GNSS for consistent positioning in variable conditions

Visual

HD cameras

360-degree camera coverage capturing route conditions, surface state, and obstacle events as the robot operates

Thermal

Thermal imaging

Thermal sensing for surface temperature variation, icy zone detection, and condition monitoring in low-visibility or night deployments

3D scanning

LiDAR

Point-cloud scanning for obstacle detection, surface geometry sensing, and clearance monitoring along the route

Radar

Radar sensing

Obstacle detection and distance monitoring for objects not visible to cameras or LiDAR in adverse conditions

Subsurface (winter)

Snow Penetrating Radar

Sub-surface sensing during winter operations to detect objects under snow accumulation

Weather

Hyper-local weather

Site-condition weather data associated with each deployment for contextual record-keeping

03 — What Snowbotix Captures

Site intelligence created as the robot works.

Snowbotix does not dispatch a separate inspection pass. During every maintenance mission, the sensing stack generates data that becomes part of the route record. Remote supervisors monitor the feed in real time and flag items for follow-up. The system builds a location-tagged picture of site conditions over time.

Surface conditions

Route surface observation

Visual and thermal records of surface state along the route, including ice patches, drainage pooling, damage patterns, and other observable conditions captured during each mission pass.

Obstructions

Obstacle and blockage detection

When the robot detects an obstruction — vehicle, debris, fallen material, access issue — it stops, alerts the supervisor, and creates a record with timestamp, GPS coordinates, and camera capture.

Hazard events

Hazard documentation workflow

Exceptions encountered during a mission follow a structured workflow: detect, capture, alert, supervisor review, site notification, record. All steps are logged with associated media.

Route patterns

Recurring condition patterns

Over multiple deployments, GPS route history and associated records begin to show where conditions consistently occur — helping facilities teams prioritize follow-up work and allocate maintenance resources.

Before/after

Pre- and post-service imagery

Geotagged images at key route points capture the state before and after service, providing a visual record that supports work order review, vendor verification, and condition tracking.

Infrastructure proximity

Route-adjacent asset observations

The sensing stack logs what it observes along the route perimeter — vegetation overgrowth, signage visibility, drainage condition, edge-of-path deterioration — as part of each mission record.

04 — Hazard Workflow

Exceptions go from detection to documented record.

When Snowbotix encounters a condition that requires human attention, the system follows a defined hazard workflow. The goal is to create a complete, reviewable record for each exception without requiring a supervisor to be physically present at the site.

01

Detect

Sensing stack identifies an obstruction, surface condition, or route exception during the mission

02

Capture

System captures HD image, GPS coordinates, timestamp, sensor data, and route context at the event location

03

Alert

Remote supervisor receives an alert in the fleet portal with exception type, location, and associated media

04

Review

Supervisor reviews the evidence, assesses the situation, and decides whether to override, redirect, pause, or document for follow-up

05

Notify

Site team is notified when follow-up action is required — route blocked, surface issue identified, access constraint found

06

Record

Exception is logged in the mission record with all associated media, location, timestamp, supervisor notes, and resolution status

CMMS and asset management

API exports

Mission history and route trends

Supervisor notes and follow-up flags

Human-reviewed items with resolution tracking and escalation history

Snowbotix records what its sensing stack observes during each maintenance route. This is not a substitute for dedicated structural engineering inspection, certified safety assessment, or compliance-grade facility audit. Site-condition data supports internal operational review and follow-up prioritization. Integration with specific external systems requires configuration during deployment setup.

06 — Site Fit

Best where recurring routes and observable site conditions overlap.

Strong fit

  • Commercial, institutional, municipal, industrial, energy, aviation, or government sites
  • Sites with recurring outdoor maintenance routes that also need condition monitoring
  • Large footprints where manual walkthroughs miss conditions between visits
  • Sites with a need for GPS-linked service records and exception documentation
  • Teams that want to connect maintenance activity to work order or CMMS workflows
  • Organizations managing distributed sites where on-site oversight is limited
  • Facilities teams that need condition data to prioritize maintenance and repair allocation

Outside Snowbotix scope

  • Structural engineering inspection or structural safety certification
  • Compliance-grade ADA or code auditing
  • Subsurface infrastructure or utility inspection
  • Rooftop, elevated, or indoor inspection
  • Hazardous-area inspection requiring certified instruments or personnel
  • Real-time automated work order generation without configuration and testing
  • Sites without a recurring outdoor maintenance route where sensing would be active
