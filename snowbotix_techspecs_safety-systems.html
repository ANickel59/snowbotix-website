Technology Specifications / Safety Systems

Supervised autonomy built for public and commercial operating environments.

Snowbotix safety architecture combines a 360° sensing envelope, a 6-foot active safety buffer, dual emergency stops, remote override, multi-level alert routing, and human-in-the-loop supervision to support controlled autonomous operation in commercial environments where people, vehicles, and equipment share the same outdoor space.

01 — Core Safety Metrics

Three numbers that govern autonomous operation in shared environments.

Safety buffer radius

6 ft

Active safety perimeter around the robot during autonomous operation. Intrusion into this zone triggers automated stop, log, and supervisor alert response.

Sensing visibility range

~30 ft

Detection range of the 360° combined sensing system in clear conditions. Enables early warning before objects enter the active safety buffer.

Max autonomous speed

2 mph

Regulated maximum autonomous operation speed. Supports stopping distance management relative to detected obstacle distances in commercial outdoor environments.

02 — Layered Safety Architecture

Ten safety layers, each operating independently.

No single safety layer is the sole control. Each layer functions independently and in combination with the others, so that degradation of any one component does not create a gap in the safety architecture.

LayerSystemFunction
Layer 01Geofenced boundariesGPS-coordinate and marker-defined operating zones that prevent the robot from leaving approved areas
Layer 02360° sensingFull perimeter LiDAR, camera, radar, and thermal coverage in all directions for obstacle detection beyond the safety buffer
Layer 036-foot safety bufferActive exclusion zone. Any detected intrusion triggers immediate stop, event log, and supervisor alert
Layer 042 mph speed regulationAutonomous speed is regulated to 2 mph to support stopping distance management relative to detected object proximity
Layer 05Physical E-stopHardware emergency stop accessible on the robot chassis for on-site crew shutdown
Layer 06Digital E-stop / remote overrideFleet portal and app controls allow supervisors to pause, redirect, or stop the robot remotely at any time
Layer 07Multi-level alert routingVisual, audio, supervisor notification, and site-broadcast alert escalation as hazard severity increases
Layer 08Auto-return to baseOn loss of communication, low battery, or certain fault conditions, the robot returns to its geotagged dock automatically
Layer 09Human-in-the-loop monitoringRemote supervisor has full visibility into position, sensor state, and live camera feeds throughout every mission
Layer 10Pre-deployment site reviewRisk assessment, route validation, exclusion zone mapping, and crew coordination protocols completed before any autonomous operation begins

03 — Speed Governance

Speed is set by operating mode, not operator preference.

Operating modeSpeedGoverning logic
Autonomous mode2 mph regulated maximumSpeed is system-regulated during autonomous operation. Cannot be overridden by the supervisor to exceed this limit during autonomous route execution.
Line-of-sight mode5–6 mph maximumApplies during crew-assisted repositioning, complex maneuvers, and deployment logistics. Requires trained operator within direct line of sight.
Docked / standbyNo movementRobot is stationary at geotagged dock or staging position. Charging or awaiting dispatch.

04 — Stop-Log-Alert Response

What happens when the sensing system detects an obstacle or hazard.

EventAutomated responseSupervisor action
Object detected within ~30 ft rangeSpeed modulation, tracking, early warning registeredAlert posted in fleet portal. Supervisor monitors.
Object within 6-foot safety bufferImmediate stop. Event logged with timestamp, GPS location, and sensor snapshot.Alert dispatched to supervisor. Supervisor reviews and decides: wait, redirect, or override and resume.
Physical E-stop engagedImmediate full stop. All drive systems deactivated.On-site crew engagement required to clear and restart.
Remote E-stop / pauseRobot stops in place or returns to staging depending on command issued.Supervisor manages recovery from fleet portal or Fleet App.
Communication lossAuto-return to geotagged dock initiated.Supervisor notified. Robot returns to base and awaits reconnection.
Low battery thresholdMission pauses. Auto-return to dock initiated.Fleet portal shows battery state and return status. Supervisor notified.

05 — Alert Architecture

Multi-level alert routing by event severity.

Visual alerts

Robot-mounted visual indicators signal status to nearby workers and crew. Operational, slowing, stopped, and fault states have distinct visual output.

Audio alerts

Audible alerts during autonomous movement and obstacle response, supporting crew awareness in noisy outdoor environments.

Supervisor notifications

Fleet portal and Fleet App receive push notifications for obstacle events, battery state, communication loss, mission completion, and fault conditions.

Site notifications

Configurable site-level alert routing for facility managers, operations leads, or on-call staff, based on role and notification preference settings.
