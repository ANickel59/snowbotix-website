2. The operational problem

Section headline
Outdoor maintenance does not happen in one season.

Body copy
Large sites do not only need snow removal in winter or mowing in summer. They need recurring outdoor work across every season: sidewalks cleared, paths swept, debris removed, slopes maintained, vegetation controlled, assets inspected, and work documented.

The problem is that most maintenance models are fragmented. One crew handles snow. Another handles mowing. A contractor handles sweeping. A separate process handles inspection. Reporting sits in texts, photos, spreadsheets, work orders, or vendor logs.

That creates a year-round operating problem.

Problem bullets
Crews are stretched across recurring low-complexity routes.
Seasonal equipment sits idle between use cases.
Vendors create inconsistent visibility across sites and seasons.
Hard-to-staff routes are delayed, skipped, or handled reactively.
Inspection and maintenance records are separated from the work itself.
Facilities leaders lack one operating view across outdoor conditions, route completion, and follow-up needs.

Transition copy
Snowbotix consolidates recurring outdoor work into one autonomous maintenance platform.

5. Attachments / sensing / operating model

Section headline
Configured for the season, mapped for the site, supervised in operation.

Attachments

Snowbotix uses modular front and rear attachments so one platform supports multiple outdoor jobs across the year.

Attachment categories include

Straight plow and V-plow for snow removal
Power broom for snow, sweeping, and surface cleanup
Vacuum-oriented configurations for debris use cases where appropriate
Mowing deck for turf, slopes, rough cuts, and vegetation routes
Liquid de-icing applicator for precision winter treatment
Granular spreader where visible treatment or site requirements call for it
Hopper and additional attachments by use case

Sensing

Snowbotix uses a commercial-grade sensor stack for autonomous navigation, obstacle detection, site awareness, and reporting.

Sensing and autonomy includes

360-degree obstacle detection
RTK-GNSS positioning
IMU-supported navigation
HD cameras
Thermal sensing
LiDAR
Onboard weather and site-condition sensing where configured
Human-in-the-loop supervision
Remote pause and override
Geofenced routes and operating zones

Operating model

Snowbotix starts with site assessment and mapping. Routes, zones, hazards, charging needs, priority areas, and operating rules are defined before deployment. The robot then runs scheduled or on-demand missions under supervised autonomy.

Operating workflow

Map the site.
Define routes, surfaces, and priority zones.
Match attachments to seasonal work.
Deploy autonomous missions.
Monitor fleet activity through the dashboard.
Capture proof-of-service records.
Review exceptions and follow-up work.

Support copy

6. Proof and reporting

Every route creates a service record.

Body copy
Snowbotix does more than perform outdoor work. It documents what happened. Each mission creates a route-level operating record that shows where the robot operated, when service started and stopped, what route it followed, what work was completed, and what exceptions need review.

Routes that change too frequently to support repeatable autonomous operation.
