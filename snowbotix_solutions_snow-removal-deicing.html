Snow Removal & De-Icing

Automate high-priority winter routes with supervised autonomous snow removal.

Snowbotix automates recurring snow removal and de-icing on defined commercial routes — sidewalks, entrances, plazas, parking areas, access paths, and emergency routes. Supervised autonomy, 360-degree sensing, human-in-the-loop monitoring, and proof-of-service records on every mission.

01 — The Winter Maintenance Problem

High-priority winter routes cannot wait for full staffing.

Sidewalk clearing, entrance maintenance, and de-icing start before most shifts begin. Emergency access routes need to be clear before weather events escalate. Parking areas, paths between buildings, and pedestrian corridors need repeated passes through sustained snowfall. The gap between when winter maintenance needs to happen and when crews are available creates real risk on commercial sites.

Snowbotix automates recurring winter routes within defined zones, operating autonomously within pre-mapped areas under continuous human supervision. The robot runs frequent passes as snow accumulates, logs exceptions when it encounters obstacles, and creates a service record after every mission.

02 — Snow Removal & De-Icing Capabilities

Configured for the winter work your site needs.

Snowbotix supports a range of winter attachment configurations depending on route type, surface, accumulation depth, and de-icing requirement. Attachment selection and final configuration are determined during site assessment.

Plowing

Snow plowing

Multiple plow widths available for sidewalks, paths, plazas, parking areas, and access routes. Snowbotix handles accumulation up to 24 in. on configured routes.

Blowing

Snow blowing

Snow blower attachment for clearing routes where pushing or windrowing is not practical, including narrow paths or routes with restricted discharge areas.

Brooming

Power brooming

Power broom for light snow cleanup, surface residue, and surface preparation ahead of de-icing application. Also used post-winter for sand and salt cleanup.

Solid de-icing

Granular material application

Salt hopper for granular de-icing material on configured routes. Refill notification sent when hopper nears capacity. Refill requires site personnel.

Liquid de-icing

Liquid de-icing

Liquid de-icing application for precision surface treatment, pretreatment workflows, and anti-icing on priority routes. Up to 30% reduction in material use compared to broadcast granular application.

Pretreatment

Anti-icing and pretreatment

Liquid Chemical Compound (LCC) pretreatment on forecast-triggered or scheduled passes to inhibit bonding before accumulation begins. 1 gallon LCC replaces approximately 50 lbs of rock salt on applicable surfaces.

De-icing precision note: Snowbotix applies de-icing material on a mapped route rather than broadcasting across a broad area. This approach reduces over-application on configured routes. Exact material savings depend on route, surface, application rate, and local conditions — site assessment determines the practical range for your site.

03 — How It Works for Snow

Frequent mapped passes through active winter conditions.

Snowbotix does not wait for accumulation to stop before running. The robot is configured for frequent recurring passes on mapped routes during active snowfall, keeping high-priority surfaces ahead of accumulation rather than clearing after the fact. Human supervisors monitor remotely throughout each mission with full override capability.

Priority routes first

Entrances, emergency access routes, and high-traffic pedestrian zones are configured as priority zones and run first in the operating sequence.

Operates during active snowfall

Snowbotix is designed for operation during snowfall. The robot runs recurring passes to manage accumulation as conditions develop rather than waiting until after a storm.

Geofenced to approved zones

Every mission runs within pre-mapped, approved routes. Exclusion zones are defined during setup. The robot cannot operate outside authorized areas.

Obstacle response

When an obstacle blocks a route, Snowbotix stops, alerts the remote supervisor, creates a record, and can be directed to resume or return to dock. Supervisor override is always available.

Refill notifications

When the salt hopper or liquid tank approaches capacity, the system notifies the site team for manual refill before the next mission window.

Proof-of-service per mission

GPS route trace, mission start and stop times, area covered, before/after media, obstacle events, and supervisor notes created at the end of every mission.

06 — What Snow Removal Is Not

Built for defined commercial routes — not broad road plowing.

Snowbotix is designed for recurring outdoor maintenance routes on commercial, institutional, municipal, industrial, and government sites. There are deployments where traditional equipment is more efficient and appropriate.

Strong fit

  • Sidewalks, pedestrian paths, and building entrances
  • Emergency access and priority routes
  • Campus walkways, courtyards, and quads
  • Parking lot edges, drive lanes, and pedestrian corridors
  • Plazas and hardscaped public-facing zones
  • Controlled access routes at industrial, energy, and government sites
  • Routes requiring repeated passes during active snowfall
  • Sites where proof-of-service documentation is needed

Less well suited

  • Broad road plowing or highway snow operations
  • Sites without a staging, charging, or supervision model
  • Residential properties
  • One-time or event-only clearing without recurring routes
  • Extreme weather requiring equipment beyond the robot's operating envelope
  • Routes that change too frequently to support pre-mapped autonomous operation
  • Very high-volume snow events requiring bulk displacement capacity
