Why Now

Three structural forces are converging to make autonomous outdoor maintenance commercially viable today.

FORCE 01

Labor Shortage, Structural

Commercial outdoor maintenance is chronically understaffed. The workforce is aging out with no replacement pipeline. This is a 10-year tailwind, not a cycle.

FORCE 02

Robotics Maturity, Ready

Autonomous navigation, sensor fusion, and modular hardware have crossed the threshold for commercial outdoor deployment. Cost curves are favorable.

FORCE 03

Buyer Demand, Proven

Facilities managers, municipal operators, and enterprise procurement teams actively seek alternatives. OpEx-based models are preferred over capital purchases.

Technology & Competitive Position

Why Snowbotix is defensible and difficult to replicate quickly.

Proprietary AIB Autonomy Stack

Purpose-built for outdoor, unstructured environments. Navigation and perception designed for snow, mud, slopes, and variable terrain, not adapted from indoor robotics.

Modular Hardware Platform (SMUR)

Single chassis with swappable attachments for snow, mowing, sweeping, and spreading. Year-round utilization on one platform reduces unit economics risk.

Site-Specific Route Intelligence

Every deployment builds a proprietary site map and operational profile. Data compounds over time, creating switching costs and operational lock-in.

Full-Stack Service Model

Snowbotix owns deployment, maintenance, software, and support. No integrator, no channel dependency. Direct relationship with the site creates durable retention.
