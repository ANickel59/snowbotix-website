Common questions

Is Snowbotix fully autonomous?

Snowbotix operates autonomously on approved, mapped routes, with human-in-the-loop monitoring, 360-degree sensing, and escalation protocols for obstructions or unusual site conditions.

How does Snowbotix know where to go?

Routes are mapped and geofenced during site assessment. The robot follows pre-approved paths and boundaries, with exclusion zones defined before deployment.

How does Snowbotix handle people, vehicles, and obstacles?

360-degree sensing detects people, vehicles, and obstructions along the route. The robot stops or reroutes and alerts the remote supervisor, who can resume, redirect, or take manual control.

How is the robot powered, charged, and stored?

Snowbotix is all-electric, returning to a wireless charging dock between missions. Docking and staging requirements are confirmed during site assessment.

What happens if support is needed?

Remote supervisors monitor missions in real time and can pause, redirect, or escalate to on-site contacts. Snowbotix also provides training, maintenance, and ongoing operational support.

What is included in the monthly service?

The managed service includes the robot platform, seasonal attachments, route mapping, fleet software, maintenance, remote support, training, and proof-of-service reporting. Final scope is confirmed after site assessment.
