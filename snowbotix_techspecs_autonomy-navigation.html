Technology Specifications / Autonomy & Navigation

Mapped-route autonomy for commercial outdoor maintenance.

Snowbotix uses a fused navigation stack combining RTK-GNSS, tactical-grade IMU, LiDAR, radar, Snow Penetrating Radar, HD cameras, thermal sensing, and hyper-local weather data to execute mapped routes in outdoor commercial environments. All autonomous operation is bounded by approved zones, supervised by remote human operators, and logged for proof-of-service records.

01 — Navigation Stack

Eight sensor and data sources operating as one system.

Snowbotix does not rely on a single sensor for navigation. The platform fuses multiple positioning and perception inputs into a coherent navigation picture. When one input degrades due to weather, terrain, or signal conditions, the fusion stack adjusts without stopping the mission.

01

RTK-GNSS

High-precision GNSS positioning for defined outdoor routes. RTK correction supports accurate positioning in open-sky conditions for repeatable, consistent route execution.

Supports defined-route repeatability across commercial sites, parking lots, sidewalk networks, and grounds maintenance routes where sky visibility is adequate.

02

Tactical-grade IMU

Inertial measurement unit for velocity tracking, tilt detection, platform orientation, and dead-reckoning in GNSS-degraded environments.

Provides heading stability and position estimation during brief positioning dropouts. IMU-only navigation is time-limited and site-validated, not a standard operational claim.

03

HD cameras

High-definition cameras for visual navigation, object classification, surface observation, and proof-of-service media capture during autonomous operation.

Camera feeds are logged to the fleet portal, viewable live by supervisors, and stored as mission records for reporting and incident review.

04

Thermal sensing

Infrared/thermal imaging for low-visibility environments, nighttime route execution, and hot-spot identification on surfaces in early freeze conditions.

Supports scheduled off-hour operations and conditions where visible-light cameras have reduced effectiveness.

05

LiDAR

Lidar scanning for 3D environment mapping, obstacle detection, route boundary verification, and high-precision object detection around the robot's operating perimeter.

Contributes to the 360° sensing envelope and supports both navigation accuracy and the 6-foot safety buffer response system.

06

Radar / Snow Penetrating Radar

Surface and subsurface sensing for object detection through snow cover, icy conditions, and low-visibility scenarios where optical and thermal sensing are reduced.

Snow Penetrating Radar detects objects and surface hazards masked by snow accumulation, supporting consistent navigation in active accumulation conditions.

07

360° sensing envelope

Full perimeter coverage combining LiDAR, camera, radar, and thermal inputs for consistent obstacle detection in all directions around the platform.

Supports the 6-foot safety bubble response system and remote human oversight. Feeds hazard alerts to supervisors and the fleet portal.

08

Hyper-local weather sensing

Integrated weather data for route scheduling, deicing trigger logic, and mission hold decisions based on site-specific temperature, precipitation, and accumulation conditions.

Feeds automatic deicing activation logic (34°F trigger threshold) and storm-response scheduling. Viewable in fleet portal dashboard.

03 — Operating Modes

Three supervised operating states.

Autonomous mode

Route execution within approved boundaries

The robot follows pre-mapped routes within defined geofenced zones at 2 mph. Human supervisor monitors remotely via fleet portal with full override capability at any time.

Line-of-sight mode

Crew-assisted movement up to 5–6 mph

Used for repositioning, access route transitions, complex maneuvers, and deployment logistics. Requires trained operator in direct line of sight.

Docked / standby mode

Charging, staging, and mission-ready hold

Platform returns to geotagged dock or staging zone between missions. Battery charges while connected to wireless dock. System maintains mission readiness for scheduled or on-demand dispatch.

GPS-degraded environments: Snowbotix does not claim standard GPS-denied autonomous operation. Operation in environments with persistent GNSS signal degradation — dense urban canyons, underground structures, heavy canopy cover, or GNSS-jamming environments — is evaluated during site assessment. Any GPS-constrained operation requires site-specific validation before deployment.

04 — Mission Workflow

Eight steps from pre-mapping to GIS outputs.

01

Pre-deployment site mapping

Physical site survey to map operating zones, identify boundaries, mark exclusion zones, set charging station locations, and define priority routes.

02

Route programming and review

Mapped routes are programmed and reviewed by the operator. Boundaries, exclusion zones, and priority logic are set in the fleet portal before any autonomous operation begins.

03

Dispatch authorization

Missions are dispatched manually or via schedule. Dispatch checks route validity, weather conditions, battery state, and attachment configuration before authorizing launch.

04

Autonomous route execution

Robot executes approved route within geofenced boundaries at 2 mph. 360° sensing active throughout. Position, sensor data, and media logged continuously.

05

Live supervisor monitoring

Human supervisor monitors position, battery, sensor alerts, and camera feeds from the fleet portal. Override available at any time to pause, redirect, or stop the mission.

06

Obstacle and hazard handling

When sensing detects an obstacle within the 6-foot safety buffer, the robot stops, logs the event, and sends an alert to the supervisor for review and decision.

07

Mission completion and return

On route completion or low-battery threshold, the platform returns to its geotagged dock or staging zone for charging and mission closeout.

08

Proof-of-service records and GIS outputs

Mission records, GPS traces, timestamped geotagged media, and sensor logs are available in the fleet portal. GIS outputs exportable as ArcGIS feature classes, GeoJSON, and API-accessible data.
