02 — Snow Removal Attachments

Plow and broom configurations for winter routes.

Straight Plow / V-Blade

4–7 ft plow configurations

Straight blade and V-blade options for mapped snow-clearing routes. Gravity-assisted down pressure with tripping spring mechanism designed to navigate pavement variances. Suited for sidewalks, campus connectors, loading access, and priority walkways.

Power Broom

50–60 in. integrated power broom

Active down-pressure where configured. Supports light snow removal, surface finish work, post-storm cleanup, and salt residue clearing. Optimized for zero-tolerance surfaces and post-clearing path conditioning.

Route Fit

Site-specific snow planning

Snow capability depends on snow depth, density, route geometry, pass frequency, attachment configuration, and service-level requirements. Route and attachment planning happens during site assessment.

03 — Material Application Systems

Winter deicing and approved year-round seasonal applications.

Snowbotix supports configurable liquid, granular, and hopper-based material application for winter deicing and approved spring, summer, and fall grounds-maintenance materials. Final material selection is validated by route, surface, refill logistics, local requirements, and attachment compatibility.

Liquid tank

30–100 gal

Liquid deicing, anti-icing pre-treatment, approved seasonal liquid applications

Granular spreader

200 lb

Salt, traction material, seed, fertilizer, soil amendments, approved granular materials

Hopper

50 gal

Seasonal dry-material handling for approved route-based applications

Material compatibility note: Final material selection must be validated before deployment. Flow behavior, particle size, corrosion exposure, tank compatibility, environmental rules, surface type, drainage, refill logistics, and local policy all affect what can be applied. Not every material is compatible with every attachment or site.

06 — Runtime by Work Mode

Mission planning by attachment type.

Runtime depends on attachment, route geometry, slope, surface condition, material load, weather, snow depth, vegetation density, and stop frequency. Use these ranges for mission planning, not as fixed operating guarantees.
