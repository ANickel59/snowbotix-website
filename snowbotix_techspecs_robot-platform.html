Technology Specifications / Robot Platform

Commercial-grade robotic chassis for year-round outdoor maintenance.

Snowbotix SMUR™ is an all-electric, autonomous, multi-utility robotic platform engineered for recurring commercial outdoor maintenance routes, including snow removal, sweeping, and steep-slope mowing. Built for high-uptime environments, the platform combines an industrial tracked chassis, modular attachments, supervised autonomy, and proof-of-service records in one year-round outdoor maintenance system.

01 — What the Platform Is

One chassis. Snow, mow, and sweep.

Snowbotix is built around the SMUR™ base unit (current generation SMUR 1.2A/B), a ruggedized electric robotic chassis designed to carry multiple seasonal implements on one commercial-grade platform. The chassis houses the drive-by-wire systems, power distribution, and battery management, executing commands from the onboard Autonomy-in-a-Box (AIB) compute — the brain of the system. Instead of separate single-purpose machines for snow, sweeping, and vegetation work, operators can deploy one robotic base across winter, spring cleanup, summer mowing, and debris-management routes.

The platform is designed for environments where outdoor maintenance is repetitive, physically demanding, time-sensitive, and hard to staff: commercial campuses, hospitals, airports, logistics yards, municipal facilities, defense installations, and contractor-managed service routes.

03 — Physical Build

Mass, traction, and surface contact matter in real outdoor work.

Outdoor maintenance is not a clean lab environment. Wet snow, freeze-thaw cycles, granular material, slush, uneven pavement, curbs, grades, and debris all reduce the usefulness of lightweight machines.

Snowbotix uses an approximately 1,500 lb base platform, increasing to approximately 2,000 lb when loaded with deicing materials or attachments. That mass supports traction, implement contact, and stability across commercial routes where lighter residential-grade units may lack the physical force required for heavy snow, power brooming, or slope work.

Traction

Track propulsion + mass

Tracks and chassis weight help the robot maintain contact on snow, ice, slopes, and uneven surfaces.

Implement pressure

Gravity-assisted contact

Platform mass supports surface contact and traction. Plow contact is gravity-assisted; broom configurations can provide active down-pressure where configured.

Stability

Commercial route durability

Weight and tracked propulsion support work around embankments, retention ponds, sidewalks, and high-use commercial routes.

04 — Mobility

Built for constrained routes, not just open lots.

The SMUR™ platform is designed for routes that include narrow passages, sidewalk networks, loading areas, emergency access paths, hydrants, signage, and mapped exclusion zones. Zero-turn maneuvering supports tight route geometry where conventional equipment may require repeated manual repositioning.

05 — All-Season Architecture

A year-round asset, not a seasonal machine.

The SMUR™ platform is designed as a single power unit for multiple outdoor maintenance jobs. The base chassis supports modular implements for snow removal, deicing, sweeping, and mowing. This increases asset utilization across seasons and reduces dependence on equipment that sits idle for much of the year.
