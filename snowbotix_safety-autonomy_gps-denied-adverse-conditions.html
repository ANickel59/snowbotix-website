GPS-Denied & Adverse Conditions

Supervised autonomy for weather, visibility, and signal-constrained routes.

The outdoor maintenance problem is worst when conditions are worst. Snowbotix is designed for routes that need to be cleared during active weather, before daylight, and in environments where signal quality is not always consistent. Site-level sensing, onboard weather data, and human-in-the-loop oversight give teams the information to make informed decisions about when and how to operate.

01 — Adverse-Condition Sensing

Onboard sensing built for outdoor maintenance conditions.

Snowbotix uses a combination of onboard sensors that are designed for outdoor maintenance environments rather than controlled indoor conditions. Snow, low visibility, and variable weather are part of the operating context — not exceptions to it.

Sensing

Snow-penetrating radar

Helps the unit detect surface conditions and obstacles beneath or within snow cover, supporting more accurate route tracking during active accumulation events.

Sensing

Hyperlocal weather sensing

Onboard weather sensors capture temperature, accumulation rate, and surface condition data tied to the specific location and time of the mission.

Sensing

360-degree perimeter sensing

The sensing perimeter around the unit remains active during adverse weather. Obstacle detection does not require clear visibility in the travel direction only.

Threshold

Temperature-based de-icing logic

Material application is triggered by a 34°F threshold. De-icing does not run continuously — it activates when surface conditions indicate it is useful and stops when conditions change.

Visibility

Mission portal monitoring

Supervisors receive live route progress, sensor readings, location, and alerts during adverse-condition missions. Human oversight remains active during the hardest operating conditions.

Endurance

Extended storm endurance

Snowbotix is designed to operate over long storm events — not just the first pass. Docking, charging, and refilling logistics are planned during site setup to support multi-pass operation.

02 — De-Icing Threshold

Material application tied to a defined temperature threshold.

De-icing activation logic

Threshold-based control reduces material waste and applies treatment when surface conditions indicate it is effective.

≤ 34°F

De-icing activated

When surface temperature readings indicate conditions at or below 34°F, the de-icing application system activates on appropriate route segments.

> 34°F

De-icing not activated

Above threshold, de-icing application is held. The robot continues route clearing without applying material where it would not be effective.

Route-level

Application tied to surface and segment

De-icing logic can be configured at the route segment level — not all surfaces receive the same treatment. Sensitive surfaces, specialty coatings, and high-sensitivity areas can be excluded or adjusted.

03 — Operating Decision Framework

Run, stop, switch modes, escalate, or defer: a five-option response framework.

Adverse conditions do not always mean a mission stops. The operating decision framework gives supervisors and teams structured options for how to respond to changing conditions during a mission.

04 — Signal-Constrained Environments

Signal quality is evaluated during site assessment.

Some sites have areas where signal quality is inconsistent — underground access, enclosed outdoor structures, dense building surroundings, and remote industrial locations. Signal-constrained conditions affect how the system operates and what capabilities are available during a mission.

Important: Snowbotix does not claim GPS-denied capability as a standard feature. Signal-constrained areas are identified and evaluated during the site assessment process. Operating in areas with degraded signal requires a specific review of what the unit can and cannot do in those conditions. This should be confirmed as part of deployment planning — not assumed from product descriptions.

Site assessment role

Signal quality is mapped during site assessment. Areas with degraded signal are identified, and operating options — including exclusion, manual-only, or deferred segments — are reviewed before deployment.

Operating options in constrained areas

Line-of-sight manual operation does not require network connectivity between the unit and the remote supervisor. For signal-constrained segments, manual operation may be the appropriate mode.

05 — Off-Hour Storm Response

Routes that need to run before crews or tenants arrive.

The most demanding outdoor maintenance window is often 2 a.m. to 6 a.m. — after accumulation has stopped or peaked, but before facilities need to be open and usable. Snowbotix is designed for this window, with event-triggered mode, supervisor-monitored operation, and proof records that document what happened before the day shift starts.

Event-triggered dispatch

Routes activate when accumulation or weather thresholds are crossed — not on a fixed clock that may not match actual conditions.

Remote supervisor oversight

Off-hour missions are monitored remotely. No crew needs to be on site for autonomous operation to proceed in approved zones.

Pre-open service records

Geocoded proof of what was cleared, when, and under what conditions — before the facility team or tenants arrive for the day.

FAQ

Questions about adverse conditions and GPS-constrained operation.

Can Snowbotix operate during active snowfall?

Snowbotix is designed to operate during active maintenance weather events. Sensor performance, route quality, and specific operating conditions should be reviewed during site assessment to confirm what the deployment can support at your location.

Does snow-penetrating radar mean the robot can see through any snow depth?

Snow-penetrating radar helps the unit detect surface conditions and obstacles within snow cover. It does not mean the robot can navigate through unlimited accumulation. Operating thresholds for snow depth and accumulation rates should be confirmed during site assessment.

What is the de-icing threshold and why 34°F?

De-icing material is most effective when surface temperatures are at or below freezing. The 34°F threshold gives a small buffer below the 32°F freezing point to account for surface variability. Application above this threshold adds material cost without proportionate benefit.

Does Snowbotix work in GPS-denied environments?

GPS-constrained areas are evaluated during the site assessment. Snowbotix does not claim GPS-denied capability as a standard feature. If your site has areas with degraded or intermittent signal, this should be reviewed before deployment to identify what operating options are appropriate for those segments.

What happens if weather conditions change during a mission?

The supervisor monitoring the mission receives sensor data and alerts in real time. If conditions change beyond operating thresholds, the supervisor can stop the mission, defer specific segments, switch to manual operation, or escalate to facility contacts.

Can the robot handle a multi-hour storm event?

Snowbotix is designed for extended storm coverage, not just a single pass. Docking, charging, and material refilling logistics are planned during site setup. For long events, the operating model supports multiple passes over the same priority routes during and after a storm.

Can Snowbotix run overnight without a crew on site?

Autonomous operation does not require a crew to be on site. Remote supervisor monitoring supports off-hour missions. Site conditions, charging infrastructure, and supervisor availability for escalation should be confirmed during deployment planning.

What records are created during adverse-condition missions?

Service records capture route progress, sensor readings, weather data, surface conditions, de-icing material use, stop events, and supervisor notes for each mission. These records are timestamped and geocoded regardless of whether conditions were routine or adverse.
