## Mandatory Style Rules

These rules apply to all copy on the site — headlines, body copy, CTAs, FAQ answers, captions, and placeholder text.

1. **Lead with the actual point.** No generic setup or throat-clearing. If the sentence does not say something specific in the first clause, rewrite it.

2. **Use plain, direct language.** Write for a facilities manager, operations director, or contractor who needs to make an operational decision. Do not write for a tech press audience.

3. **Prefer concrete nouns, buyer language, operational details, constraints, and proof.** Say "snow removal from walkways and entrance zones" not "autonomous outdoor maintenance solutions."

4. **Avoid generic AI phrasing, filler, hype, and empty differentiation.** If the sentence could appear on the homepage of any AI, robotics, or SaaS company, rewrite it with something specific to Snowbotix's buyer, workflow, or product.

5. **Avoid stacked modifiers and vague transformation language.** "Powerful platform," "cutting-edge autonomy," "seamless integration," "robust solution," "transformative technology" — none of these say anything. Cut them.

6. **Do not use the following words or phrases unless evidence specifically justifies them:** revolutionary, cutting-edge, seamless, robust, powerful platform, transform, unlock, elevate, game-changing, disruptive, best-in-class, industry-leading, world-class.

7. **Avoid em dashes.** Use commas, periods, colons, or cleaner sentence structure instead. Do not remove necessary technical hyphens or standard compound terms (e.g., "proof-of-service," "all-electric," "modular attachments").

8. **If a sentence sounds polished but says little, rewrite it** with a concrete buyer problem, workflow detail, product capability, or proof point.

9. **Do not invent facts, customer claims, performance claims, specifications, or outcomes.** Every hard number, customer reference, and outcome statement must appear in the approved claims list in this file.

10. **Do not make copy warmer, punchier, or more dramatic by changing the claim.** Style is not a reason to overstate.

11. **Keep writing specific to Snowbotix's buyer, workflow, operating environment, and actual product evidence.** The site should not read like it was written for a generic robotics or SaaS company.
