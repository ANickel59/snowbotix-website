## What We Are Building

Snowbotix is a robotics-as-a-service company deploying autonomous outdoor maintenance robots on commercial, institutional, industrial, and government sites.

Our work combines hardware, software, and service into a single deployable system:

### Hardware

Commercial-grade robots and modular attachments for outdoor environments, including snow, sweeping, mowing, and site maintenance work.

### Software

Autonomy, route configuration, real-time monitoring, and proof-of-service reporting designed for operational accountability.

### Service

Site assessment, deployment support, training, maintenance, and field operations support so customers can adopt autonomy without owning the full technical burden.

## Who We Look For

We look for people who can operate in the gap between engineering ambition and real-world deployment.

The work is practical. The environment is changing. The details matter.

Strong candidates tend to be:

- Builders who want their work to move in the physical world.
- Operators who can solve problems without waiting for perfect conditions.
- Engineers and technicians who care about reliability, safety, and field performance.
- Commercial team members who can work with complex buyers and high-stakes operating environments.
- People who communicate clearly, document what they learn, and improve the system as they go.

## How We Work

Snowbotix is built by a team with backgrounds in robotics, autonomous systems, commercial operations, and enterprise software.

We deploy what we build. That means the work does not stop at a prototype, demo, or slide deck. The system has to perform in live operating environments, with customers, weather, terrain, equipment, and safety constraints all in play.

### Our working principles

**Build for real-world sites.**  
We design for outdoor conditions, operational constraints, and the people responsible for keeping sites running.

**Document the work.**  
Reliable operations require clear records, route evidence, deployment notes, customer context, and technical follow-through.

**Respect the field.**  
Robotics only matters if it makes work safer, more reliable, or easier to manage for real operators.

**Improve the system.**  
Every deployment should make the product, process, and customer experience stronger.
