Liability, Compliance & Risk Controls

Documented outdoor maintenance for high-accountability sites.

Facilities with high liability exposure — healthcare campuses, airports, logistics yards, municipalities, defense installations — need more than cleared routes. They need a record of what happened. Snowbotix creates an operating record tied to location, time, route, conditions, and events. That record supports safety review, operations accountability, and documentation for the stakeholders who ask the hard questions after an incident.

01 — What Snowbotix Is and Is Not

A tool for documentation and operating controls.

Documentation matters. When something goes wrong on a commercial or industrial site, the first questions are typically the same. Snowbotix is designed to have answers to those questions in the operating record.

Q1

Where was the service performed?

Geocoded service records tie route completion to specific location data. The operating record shows which segments were serviced and which were not.

Q2

When was the service performed?

Every record is timestamped. Mission start, segment completion, stop events, material application, and supervisor actions are all tied to a time reference.

Q3

What route was covered?

Route completion records show what was completed and what was not. Incomplete segments — and the reason — are logged rather than absent from the record.

Q4

What conditions were present?

Onboard sensor data captures weather, temperature, surface conditions, and accumulation at the time and location of the mission. These are not retroactive estimates — they are readings tied to the operating window.

Q5

What interrupted the service?

Stop events, obstacle detections, escalation actions, and supervisor decisions are logged with location, time, and reason. The record reflects what happened, not just what was planned.

03 — Proof-of-Service Record

What gets captured on every mission.

Geocoded before-and-after photos

Visual evidence tied to location, route segment, and timestamp for completed service areas.

Route completion log

Completed segments, incomplete segments, reason for incompletion, and mission duration.

Stop and obstacle events

Each stop event with location, trigger type, timestamp, sensor data, and supervisor review action.

Weather and sensor data

Temperature, accumulation, surface conditions, and visibility readings tied to the mission window.

Material application records

De-icing material use by route segment, quantity, threshold trigger data, and surface type where applicable.

Supervisor notes and escalations

Human review actions, facility notifications, rerouting decisions, and deferred segment notes.

04 — Pre-Deployment Risk Controls

Controls applied before the robot starts work support the operating framework.

Risk management starts before the first mission. The deployment process includes a set of pre-deployment controls that establish the operating framework within which the robot works.

Site assessment and route approval

Routes reviewed and approved by the facility team before autonomous operation begins. Operating boundaries reflect actual site conditions.

Geofencing and exclusion zone mapping

Restricted areas, sensitive surfaces, emergency access, and exclusion zones defined and mapped before deployment.

Stakeholder documentation of operating boundaries

Facility representatives review and confirm the operating map. The review is documented as part of the deployment record.

Escalation path definition

Supervisor escalation contacts, facility notification procedures, and maintenance response paths are defined before the pilot begins.

Pilot review criteria

Success and review criteria for the pilot phase are established in advance, not evaluated subjectively after the fact.

05 — Pilot-to-Scale Risk Review

Expansion is based on evidence from the pilot, not assumptions.

Snowbotix deployments expand from pilot to full scale through a structured review. Before additional routes or sites are added, the pilot record is reviewed against the criteria established before the pilot began.

Route performance: Did the robot complete priority routes consistently during the pilot period?
Stop events: What types of stop events occurred, how frequently, and were they resolved appropriately?
Escalation quality: Did the supervisor escalation process work as planned for every event that required it?
Record quality: Are the proof-of-service records complete, accurate, and accessible for the stakeholders who need them?
Stakeholder review: Has the facility team reviewed the pilot record and confirmed the operating model before expansion is approved?
Identified exclusions: Were any route segments, zones, or operating windows identified during the pilot as unsuitable that should be adjusted before expansion?

06 — ESG and Surface Records

Documentation for sustainability and surface stewardship reporting.

Snowbotix creates records that support ESG-related reporting and surface stewardship goals alongside safety and operations documentation.

Material use records

De-icing material application logged by route, surface, quantity, and threshold condition. Supports reduction-from-baseline comparisons.

Surface protection records

Sensitive surfaces, specialty coatings, and protected areas are logged separately. Application exclusions documented in the operating record.

Operational carbon data

Route coverage, energy use, and operational runtime data available for sustainability reporting workflows.

FAQ

Questions about liability, compliance, and documentation.

Does Snowbotix reduce our liability exposure?

Snowbotix creates operating records and applies pre-deployment controls that are relevant to how your risk team and legal counsel evaluate your exposure. Whether and how those records reduce specific liability depends on your jurisdiction, applicable standards, and your legal and insurance situation — which should be evaluated by your own advisors. Snowbotix is not a liability shield, and we do not make that claim.

Does Snowbotix support regulatory compliance for outdoor maintenance?

Snowbotix creates documented records of maintenance activity, conditions, and events that may be relevant to regulatory requirements for your facility. Whether the records satisfy specific compliance obligations depends on your jurisdiction and applicable regulations. Compliance determination is a legal and regulatory matter — Snowbotix records support that review, not replace it.

What if something goes wrong during a mission?

If an incident occurs during a Snowbotix mission, the operating record captures what the robot was doing, where it was, what sensors detected, when the event occurred, and what the supervisor did in response. This record is available for review. How incidents are managed, reported, and addressed legally remains the responsibility of the facility operator and their advisors.

Who owns the service records?

Service records are accessible to the facility operator through the mission visibility portal. Data retention, ownership, and export requirements should be reviewed during deployment planning as part of your data governance and records-keeping obligations.

Can service records be used in a legal proceeding?

The admissibility, relevance, and weight of operating records in legal proceedings depends on jurisdiction, the nature of the proceeding, and how the records were generated, stored, and produced. This is a question for your legal team, not a product feature claim.

What records exist if the robot does not complete a route?

Incomplete routes are logged with the reason for incompletion — stop events, escalation, supervisor deferral, or conditions exceeding operating thresholds. An incomplete mission still generates a record that shows what was attempted, what occurred, and what decision was made.

Does Snowbotix support insurance documentation requirements?

Some commercial property insurers or brokers may find documented operating records relevant to their review of outdoor maintenance practices. Whether and how Snowbotix records satisfy your specific insurance documentation requirements depends on your policy terms and your insurer. We recommend reviewing this with your broker before making coverage assumptions.

How does de-icing material documentation support risk review?

Material application records include route, surface type, quantity, threshold trigger data, and timing. This creates an audit trail for material use that is useful for reviewing whether application occurred, on which surfaces, and under what conditions — without relying on manual crew reporting.
