Liability, Compliance & Risk Controls
Documented outdoor maintenance for high-accountability sites.

Snowbotix creates a documented operating record with geocoded service evidence.

For facilities, contractors, municipalities, airports, healthcare campuses, logistics yards, and defense facilities, the risk is not only whether work gets done. The risk is whether anyone can prove what happened, when it happened, where it happened, what conditions were present, and how exceptions were handled.

Snowbotix supports that operating need through mapped routes, geofenced boundaries, 360-degree sensing, human-in-the-loop monitoring, stop-event records, supervisor escalation, mission logs, and proof-of-service reporting.

The core idea: reduce ambiguity in the operating record.

Traditional outdoor maintenance often leaves too much room for uncertainty.

A contractor says the route was serviced. A supervisor cannot verify the exact time. A walkway was blocked by a vehicle, but no one logged it. A crew skipped a segment because of a fallen branch. A snow event escalated after shift change. A surface required liquid-only treatment, but the work record is incomplete.

Snowbotix is designed to make that operating context more visible.

Risk control starts before deployment.

Snowbotix risk controls begin with site assessment, not after an incident.

The site assessment should define where Snowbotix can operate, where it should not operate, what routes matter most, and which operational records need to be captured for the customer’s review process.

Pre-deployment controls
Control	Purpose
Site assessment	Identify operating fit, site risks, routes, surfaces, and deployment constraints
Route mapping	Define approved maintenance routes before autonomous work begins
Geofenced boundaries	Keep work inside reviewed operating zones
Exclusion zones	Identify areas that should remain manual, restricted, deferred, or out of scope
Surface tagging	Identify pervious concrete, liquid-only zones, granular-allowed areas, and sensitive surfaces
Obstacle mapping	Identify curbs, speed bumps, hydrants, bollards, gates, ramps, and known hazards
Priority routing	Define which routes must be serviced first during storms or operating peaks
Escalation rules	Define who is notified when a route is blocked, interrupted, or incomplete
Proof requirements	Define which records matter for operations, safety, customer reporting, or compliance review

FAQ
Does Snowbotix eliminate liability?

No. Snowbotix does not eliminate liability. Snowbotix creates a documented operating record with geocoded service evidence, mission logs, sensor records, stop events, and supervisor notes that can support risk review and service verification.

Does Snowbotix guarantee compliance?

No. Snowbotix supports compliance workflows with documentation. Customers should review Snowbotix records against their own safety policies, legal requirements, procurement rules, insurance expectations, and regulatory obligations.

What records does Snowbotix create?

Snowbotix records can include route progress, robot location, deployment history, runtime, geocoded before-and-after evidence, area serviced, chemical application, sensor logs, stop events, obstruction events, and supervisor notes.

How does Snowbotix help with contractor oversight?

Snowbotix helps show where service was completed, where it was interrupted, and what conditions affected the route. This can support customer communication, post-event review, and contractor performance management.

Can Snowbotix records support safety reviews?

Yes. Snowbotix records can support safety reviews by documenting stop events, obstruction events, route interruptions, supervisor escalation, and geocoded service evidence.

How does Snowbotix support public-sector or regulated buyers?

Snowbotix can support public-sector and regulated buyers by defining route scope, deployment requirements, proof records, escalation workflows, service history, and reporting outputs. Specific compliance requirements should be confirmed through the buyer’s legal, procurement, and safety review processes.

Can Snowbotix document de-icing material use?

Yes. Snowbotix reporting can include chemical application records, material usage, route location, and surface-specific treatment activity where configured.

Why does human-in-the-loop monitoring matter for risk controls?

Human-in-the-loop monitoring adds supervisor review when the robot encounters conditions that require judgment, such as blocked routes, fallen debris, high-priority route interruptions, or site hazards.
