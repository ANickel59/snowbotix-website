Attachments & Capabilities
One Robot. Snow, Mow, Sweep, Apply, and Inspect.

Snowbotix is built around a modular attachment system that turns one electric robotic platform into a year-round outdoor maintenance asset.

The SMUR™ platform can be configured for snow removal, deicing, sweeping, vacuum debris collection, mowing, vegetation management, seasonal material application, and inspection while maintaining. Instead of assigning separate machines and crews to every seasonal task, operators can use one robotic base across recurring outdoor maintenance routes.

Snowbotix is designed for high-uptime environments where outdoor work is repetitive, hard to staff, physically demanding, or difficult to verify: sidewalks, trails, parks, rights-of-way, campuses, logistics yards, airfields, commercial facilities, municipal routes, and controlled-access government sites.

Built for front and rear attachment configurations

Snowbotix uses a modular tracked carrier designed for front and rear attachments. This lets the platform combine a primary work tool, such as a plow, broom, mower, or vacuum, with secondary systems such as liquid or granular material application.

That matters because many outdoor routes need more than one action in the same service window. A winter route may require clearing plus treatment. A spring route may require sweeping plus documentation. A municipal route may require debris removal, surface observation, and GIS-tagged maintenance records.

The attachment system is designed to support multi-season utilization, not single-season equipment idling.

Snow removal attachments

Snowbotix supports plow and broom configurations for autonomous winter operations on mapped commercial and public routes.

Straight plow and V-plow / V-blade

The snow plow system supports 4–7 ft straight plow configurations, including straight blade and V-blade options. These attachments are used for mapped snow-clearing routes such as sidewalks, campus connectors, loading access, priority walkways, service paths, and controlled outdoor zones.

The Technical Overview identifies Boss-integrated 4–7 ft plow configurations, including straight blade and V-blade options, with gravity-assisted down pressure and a tripping spring mechanism designed to navigate pavement variances.

Material compatibility note

Final material selection must be validated before deployment. Flow behavior, particle size, corrosion exposure, tank compatibility, environmental rules, surface type, drainage, refill logistics, and local policy all affect what can be applied.

Use this claim on the page:

Snowbotix supports configurable liquid, granular, and hopper-based material application for winter deicing and approved spring, summer, and fall grounds-maintenance materials. Final material selection is validated by route, surface, refill logistics, local requirements, and attachment compatibility.

Do not use this claim:

“The spreader handles any material.”

That is too broad.

Inspection while maintaining

Snowbotix can be configured to capture inspection data while it maintains mapped routes. This is important enough to include on the Attachments & Capabilities page, but not enough to take over the page. The deeper technical detail belongs on the future Inspection or Data pages.

As the robot clears, sweeps, vacuums, applies material, or manages vegetation, onboard systems can log site conditions and produce geotagged records. Where configured, inspection outputs can include surface defects, vegetation obstructions, icy spots, markings, ADA-route issues, geotagged records, and GIS-ready layers.
