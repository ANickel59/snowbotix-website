02 — One Platform, Multiple Outdoor Workflows

The same robot does the work across seasons.

Snowbotix is built around a single commercial-grade robotic chassis — the SMUR™ platform — that supports modular attachments, a fused navigation stack, 360-degree sensing, and remote human oversight. The operating model, routes, and proof-of-service infrastructure stay consistent as you change the season and the work.

Platform

All-electric multi-utility chassis

~1,500 lb base unit with industrial track propulsion, modular attachment architecture, zero-turn maneuverability, and 24 in. snow capability.

Sensing

360-degree fused sensing stack

RTK-GNSS, IMU, LiDAR, radar, Snow Penetrating Radar, HD cameras, thermal sensing, and hyper-local weather data operating together during every mission.

Oversight

Human-in-the-loop monitoring

Remote supervisor monitors position, battery, sensor alerts, and live camera feeds throughout every mission. Override available at any time.

Operating model

Geofenced mapped routes

Every mission runs within approved zones. Routes are pre-mapped, exclusion zones are set, and the robot cannot leave authorized areas.

Reporting

Proof-of-service records

GPS traces, timestamped geotagged media, surface observations, and event logs created per mission. Exportable as GIS, API, and CMMS outputs.

Power

LFP battery with hot-swap

~7 kW LFP battery, 4–6 hr typical runtime, ~1-min hot-swap, wireless dock on standard 110V, and up to 60 days standby capability.

04 — Fit Criteria

Built for recurring routes, not one-off jobs.

Strong fit

  • Commercial, institutional, municipal, industrial, energy, aviation, or government sites
  • Recurring outdoor maintenance routes
  • Hard-to-staff routes or stretched facilities teams
  • Multiple seasonal needs across snow, sweeping, mowing, or inspection
  • Defined sidewalks, paths, slopes, yards, lots, or controlled zones
  • Need for route-level service documentation
  • Ability to support mapping, staging, charging, and supervision
  • Internal owner to coordinate facilities, operations, or public works stakeholders

Likely not a fit

  • Residential properties
  • One-time cleanup or event-based jobs
  • Sites with no recurring outdoor maintenance route
  • Indoor-only maintenance needs
  • High-speed public road sweeping or roadway-only work
  • Bulk waste removal or hazardous material cleanup
  • Sites without a practical staging, charging, or supervision model
  • Routes that change too frequently to support repeatable autonomous operation
