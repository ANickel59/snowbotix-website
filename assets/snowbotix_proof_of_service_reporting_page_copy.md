## 10. How It Works

**Section headline**  
From mapped routes to service records.

**Step 1: Map the site**  
Snowbotix maps the routes, zones, access points, surfaces, obstacles, and priority areas that define the operating environment.

**Step 2: Define reporting needs**  
The team defines what Snowbotix records: route completion, before/after media, sensor data, exception events, supervisor notes, area covered, or export requirements.

**Step 3: Run autonomous missions**  
Snowbotix operates on approved routes using supervised autonomy, 360-degree sensing, and human-in-the-loop monitoring.

**Step 4: Capture proof-of-service records**  
The system records mission history, GPS traces, visual evidence, sensor context, route exceptions, and supervisor notes.

**Step 5: Review, export, and act**  
Teams use the dashboard and reports to verify service, review exceptions, update work orders, and plan follow-up maintenance.

# FAQ Copy

## How does Snowbotix document completed work?

Snowbotix records mission history, GPS route traces, route completion, before/after geotagged media, sensor context, exception events, and supervisor notes through the fleet dashboard.

## Does Snowbotix support before/after reporting?

Yes. Snowbotix captures before/after geotagged and timestamped photos or video during maintenance missions.

## Does the dashboard show live robot status?

Yes. The fleet dashboard shows live robot status, location, battery level, mission progress, deployment history, and service records.

## What happens when a route is blocked?

Snowbotix logs the obstruction, captures field evidence, pauses or reroutes based on the operating model, and brings the event to supervisor review. The exception record shows what happened and what needs follow-up.

## Can reports connect to existing systems?

Snowbotix supports GIS outputs, ArcGIS/GeoJSON exports, and API options where configured. Integration scope depends on the customer’s systems and deployment requirements.

## Is this only for snow removal?

No. Proof-of-service reporting supports Snowbotix maintenance routes across snow, de-icing, sweeping, debris, mowing, vegetation, and inspection workflows.

## Is this for residential properties?

No. Snowbotix is built for commercial, institutional, municipal, industrial, energy, aviation, and government sites with recurring outdoor maintenance routes.
